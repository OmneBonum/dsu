The Democracy Straight-Up! Story

This blog is for sharing my thoughts about the Democracy Straight-Up! project as it unfolds. The project will enable US voters nation-wide to vote directly on legislation before the US House of Representatives.

The idea is simply called direct legislation. We view the exercise of this kind of direct voting not as an optional tweak, but as a fundamental right that has been over-looked for too long. The reality is, laws inevitably affect freedom. Every individual in a democracy gives up some freedom--and gains a great deal of benefit, hopefully--when laws are enacts. Consequently, every individual must have a meaningful opportunity to weigh in on every law.

Fear Not

Direct legislation is not a new idea, and, to be sure, there has been a great deal of fear and misunderstanding around it whenever its implementation has been proposed. We'll try to allay any fears in advance by emphasizing what the DSUp Project is not: it does not propose legislation through referenda, direct democracy, or that every citizen must become an expert on every issue. It does not propose any changes to existing law or the constitution. The project has no political orientation--it is meant for every American voter, regardless of political affiliation.
